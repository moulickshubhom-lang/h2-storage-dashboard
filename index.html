Hydrogen Storage Technology Comparator

Nine hydrogen storage routes — compressed gas, cryogenic liquid, chemical carriers, metal hydrides and bulk geological storage — compared on the physics that actually governs their footprint and cost, benchmarked against the US DOE onboard storage targets.

Read this before comparing numbers: gravimetric capacity is reported on a system basis (gas + tank/cryostat) for compressed and liquid hydrogen, since hydrogen has no carrier material of its own — but on a neat carrier basis (excluding tank/reactor) for chemical carriers and metal hydrides. Mixing these two bases is the most common mistake in casual storage comparisons. See the "Details" column in the table for the exact basis used for each value, with sources.
